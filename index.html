Abstract

Spatial reasoning over 3D scenes often fails before reasoning begins: the relevant relation may simply not be visible from the current view. We study this evidence-selection problem in the minimal nontrivial compositional setting — a two-hop chain in which a prefix relation selects an intermediate entity and a suffix relation selects the final answer. Given an oracle relation chain and a 3D Gaussian Splatting scene memory, we ask where the system should look so that each hop becomes visually verifiable. We propose relation-conditioned canonical views Cr, analytic camera poses determined by the geometry of each spatial relation, and use lightweight per-relation discriminators Dr to score rendered evidence.

In paired comparisons across 20 ScanNet-GS scenes and three relations, Cr improves single-hop verification over random views for all relations and over strong eye-level defaults for left-right and front-back. On boundary-hard two-hop questions, where distractors lie near relation boundaries, Cr outperforms eye-level defaults by 16.9 percentage points and fixed three-view max-aggregation by 33 points. On a matched diagnostic subset, mechanism analysis localises the gain to anchor-sensitive intermediate selection: removing the prefix hop collapses both methods, while shuffling the prefix anchor hurts Cr much more than default. Auxiliary diagnostics show that the tested alternatives are not stable substitutes: parameter-Fisher is anti-correlated with answerability (ρ = −0.985 on a left-right diagnostic), while three Er evidence-channel variants either improve only in-domain, regress on both domains, or specialise to the deployed Cr-canonical eval at the cost of broad-pose robustness.

Single-Hop: Cr Generalises Across Relations

We first verify that Cr canonical views are systematically more informative than two natural baselines on single-hop relation verification. Each (a, b) pair is rendered under all view-generating methods (shared per-pair seed) and the same Dr decides the rendered image. Cr beats random-anywhere significantly across all three relations and beats default eye-level on left-right and front-back; the up-down tie is theoretically expected because gravity-axis alignment is already satisfied by an eye-level horizontal view.

Single-hop relation verification. Cluster-bootstrap mean accuracy on 20 ScanNet-GS scenes × 80 pairs/scene, paired across methods. Cr beats random-anywhere on all three relations and default on left-right and front-back.

Takeaway. Relation-conditioned geometry generalises: the perpendicular-bisector and along-AB-axis priors win where they have geometric content; up-down has no such structural lever beyond gravity alignment, exactly as predicted.

A Two-Hop Chain on a Real ScanNet Scene

For the query “what is in front of the couch that is to the right of the ottoman?”, the default overview view mixes anchor, intermediate, gold final, and a near-miss distractor in a single frame. Our pipeline instead renders the Cr-canonical view per hop: Cr-LR for hop 1 aligns the relation axis with image-x, and Cr-FB for hop 2 aligns it with camera-space depth.

Two-Hop Boundary-Hard: Cr Beats All View Baselines

The boundary-hard stratum places three distractors per query in the hop-0 negative-margin band — geometrically close to the relation boundary on the wrong side. This isolates the regime where Cr's relation-conditioned geometry should matter. On the expanded run (n = 319 queries over 12 of 20 scenes), Cr reaches 0.837 vs 0.668 for the strong eye-level default (paired-Δ = +0.169, 95% CI [+0.086, +0.240]). A separate n = 125 no-leak cohort confirms that even an oracle over three fixed orthographic views (best-of-3 per query) trails Cr by −15.3 pp.

Boundary-hard 2-hop MCQ accuracy. Cr reaches 0.837; the strong default eye-level baseline reaches 0.668; fixed 3-orthographic-view max-aggregation collapses to 0.508.

Mechanism: Anchor-Sensitive Intermediate Selection

Where does the boundary-hard gain come from? We run two pipeline ablations on the no-leak n = 125 cohort. Suffix-only skips the prefix hop entirely; if the chain were shortcut, accuracy should not change. Prefix-shuffle replaces the anchor with a random non-chain object; if the anchor is doing real work, accuracy should drop. Both methods rely on the prefix (suffix-only collapses by 50–70 pp), so neither pipeline is a shortcut. But prefix-shuffle hits Cr nearly four times harder than default (−19 pp vs −6 pp), indicating that Cr's hop-0 scores genuinely depend on the anchor — the relation-conditioned geometry is what makes the prefix matter.

Two-hop faithfulness ablations. Both pipelines are genuinely two-hop on this stratum (suffix-only collapses). Cr is much more sensitive to prefix-anchor shuffling than default — the relation-conditioned view prior makes hop-0 scores depend on the anchor.

Takeaway. The 16.9 pp boundary-hard gain is faithful: it is concentrated in intermediate-selection coverage, not in suffix verification. On a matched diagnostic subset (n = 88), Cr's top-K beam contains the gold intermediate for 74% of queries vs 61% for default; given z* in beam, default actually scores the suffix slightly better (0.944 vs 0.892).

Why Cr Resolves Single-Hop Ambiguity

The default oblique camera compresses the LR axis: the two entities project to similar image-x, so the discriminator's image-x signal is near-chance. The Cr camera sits on the perpendicular bisector of ab, looking at the midpoint; image-x then cleanly separates a from b, and the same discriminator becomes confident.

Contributions

  • C1. We isolate per-hop evidence viewpoint selection as a hidden bottleneck in 3D compositional spatial reasoning, and operationalise the minimal-nontrivial-primitive view: two hops is where viewpoint selection first affects intermediate-entity choice.
  • C2. We define analytic relation-conditioned canonical views Cr for three pairwise spatial relations — perpendicular bisector for left-right, along-AB axis for front-back, gravity-aligned eye-level for up-down. Cr requires no learning.
  • C3. Single-hop validation: Cr beats random-anywhere across all three relations and beats default eye-level on left-right and front-back, with paired CIs that exclude zero.
  • C4. Two-hop boundary-hard composition: Cr wins by +16.9 pp over default and +33 pp over fixed-3-view max-aggregation; an oracle over three fixed orthographic views still trails by −15.3 pp.
  • C5. Mechanism and negative results: ablations localise the gain to anchor-sensitive intermediate selection; FisherRF-style parameter Fisher is anti-correlated with answerability (ρ = −0.985) and three Er evidence-channel upgrades land in three different regimes that together do not yield a universal cross-domain gain.
